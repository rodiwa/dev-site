Machine Learning

Even though I'm not an expert on this subject, I'd like to believe I know enough basics to hold an intelligent conversation.

How It All Started

I admit that I started looking into Machine Learning only because it sounded cool. No other reason. I wanted to understand what the hype was about. I also probably didn't want to be left out of all the ML lingo and talk that my friends and colleagues kept having every now and then.

Having picked up on the basics of Machine Learning in the past few months, I've realised how ML can be used to solve problems in various domains. And that in itself is reason enough to fully commit to it. I still continue to pursue ML only as a technical achievement for me for now. But I'm excited at the prospect of being able to learn something new in the process.

How It's Going Right Now

Rather than jumping into advanced topics straight away, I'm focusing on the fundamentals that will hopefully help me in the long run. With an eye out for what is most widely used in the industry right now, I have a good understanding of the following topics.
